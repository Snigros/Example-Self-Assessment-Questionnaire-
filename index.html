Organisational Suitability Assessment

If you are considering a career with our organisation, we encourage you to complete a suitability self-assessment. This will ask some general questions, and based on your responses, provide some general advice. It should take approximately 10 minutes to complete.

Start Assessment


Disclaimer

This tool was intended to help you identify aspects of your life or personal circumstances that might need to be considered by our organisation as part of your pre-employment character and integrity suitability assessment. This self-assessment is completely anonymous and no personally identifiable information will be kept, recorded or stored by our organisation. Information you provide cannot be used during, or form part of our organisation's selection and recruitment processes.

As no personally identifiable information is kept, recorded or stored by our organisation, any information you provide cannot be further disclosed. Advice provided by this tool should be treated as a guide only and should not be considered conclusive. Any recommendations are intended to be used as general guidance and do not prohibit you from considering a career with our organisation.

Question 1

Are you an Australian Citizen?

Question 2

Are you over 18?

Question 3

Are you at least 15 years old and currently completing an Australian school-based apprenticeship?

Question 4

Do you act with the highest degree of integrity and honesty, both at work and in your personal life?

Question 5

Have you ever been found guilty of a criminal offence, or have you ever spent time in jail?

Question 6

Have you (or your family or friends) ever socialised or associated with people (except in the course of your official duties) who engage in criminal activity, or members of outlaw motorcycle gangs, street gangs or extremist groups?

Question 7

Have you ever used (except where the use was prescribed by a doctor) illicit drugs, performance/image enhancing drugs (e.g. steroids) or other prescription medications?

Question 8

Have you ever (except in the course of your official duties) viewed child pornography?

Question 9

Have you (or your family or friends) ever been associated with the production and/or distribution of illegal goods (such as illicit drugs, precursor chemicals, weapons, firearms, alcohol or tobacco products)?

Question 10

Have you ever had your driver's licence suspended or cancelled?

Question 11

Have you ever been the subject of an apprehended violence order, restraining order or other similar order (however described)?

Recommendation

You must be an Australian Citizen to be eligible for employment with the our organisation.

Section 22(8) of the Public Service Act 1999 states that:

An Agency Head must not engage, as an APS employee, a person who is not an Australian citizen, unless the Agency Head considers it appropriate to do so.

Consistent with this requirement, the Chief Executive Officer of our organisation has determined that is not appropriate for non-Australian citizens to be employed within the our organisation.

Recommendation

As a general rule, you must be at least 18 years old to be eligible for employment with our organisation.

However if you at least 15 years old, and currently completing an Australian School-based Apprenticeship, you may also be eligible.

Recommendation

Our employees must demonstrate and adhere to the highest standards of integrity and ethical behaviour - both on, and off, duty.

If you feel that you are unable to meet this requirement, you may not be suitable for employment with the our organisation.

Recommendation

Our employees must demonstrate respect for the law, and a commitment to upholding and enforcing the law.

A criminal conviction does not prevent you from being employed by our organisation - particularly if you have been convicted of a minor matter, or have a conviction which occurred a long time ago. However, if you have a significant or extensive criminal history, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

As a law enforcement agency, it is not appropriate for, nor does our organisation tolerate, associations between our officers and criminality. These relationships are fundamentally inconsistent with our role, mission and values.

If you knowingly and actively associate (except in the course of your official duties) with criminals or persons who may be involved in criminal activity, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

Our organisation has a 'zero tolerance' policy in relation to any officers using, possessing or selling illicit substances (prohibited drugs). This activity is fundamentally inconsistent with our role, mission and values.

If you have consumed prohibited drugs in the past, this does not prevent you from being employed by our organisation - particularly if the use was minor or it occurred a long time ago. However, if you currently use prohibited drugs, or have a significant or extensive history of drug use, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

Our agency is one of those charged with protecting the most vulnerable individuals within our society.

Accessing child exploitation material is a serious crime and is fundamentally inconsistent with our role, mission and values.

If you have ever viewed, downloaded, accessed or purchased child pornography, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

Our employees must demonstrate respect for the law, and a commitment to upholding and enforcing the law. Involvement in, or association with, unlawful activity is fundamentally inconsistent with our role, mission and values.

If you are or have been (except in the course of your official duties) involved in, or associated with the production, sale, manufacture or distribution of illegal goods, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

Our employees must demonstrate respect for the law, and a commitment to upholding and enforcing the law.

Receiving a speeding fine or other traffic infringement does not prevent you from being employed by our organisation - particularly where the infringement(s) may have been minor, committed without intent, or may have occurred a long time ago.

However, if you have a significant or extensive history of serious driving offences or traffic infringements, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

Our employees must demonstrate and adhere to the highest standards of integrity and ethical behaviour - both on, and off, duty.

If you are currently the subject of an Apprehended Violence Order, Restraining Order or other similar order, you may not be suitable for employment with our organisation.

Recommendation

Thank you for completing the our organisation character and integrity suitability self-assessment.

If you completed this self-assessment truthfully and did not receive any adverse recommendations or advice, there does not appear to be anything in your background from a character and integrity perspective which may automatically prevent you from being employed by our organisation. We encourage you to apply for positions of interest as they become available.

Please remember that if you do apply for a career with our organisation you must be open and honest in the information you provide, as a failure to do so may impact adversely on an assessment of your suitability for employment with our organisation.
